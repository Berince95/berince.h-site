About Me

I am Bérince S. R. Hounsouvo, a passionate Ph.D. candidate in Forest Sciences at Université Laval, Québec, Canada. My research focuses on forest ecology, particularly on revegetation of acid mine drainage sites using boreal woody species. I am dedicated to the restoration of ecosystems using nature-based solutions to help mitigate environmental degradation caused by human activities.

Research

My current research explores boreal woody species with lateral root architecture for use in revegetation on acid mine drainage sites. I also investigate the role of ectomycorrhizal fungi in improving plant growth and ecosystem function.
